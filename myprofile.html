About Me

I am a believer in Jesus Christ and I seek to reflect His character in my work and daily life. My professional focus is child safeguarding, advocacy for children and families, and leadership development. I work with caregivers, teachers, and community leaders to strengthen systems that protect children and support healthy family environments. Through Safe Steps Training Services I design training programs that raise awareness, build practical safeguarding skills, and help institutions develop effective child protection policies. The goal is to empower families, strengthen communities, and ensure that children grow up in environments where they feel safe, valued, and respected.

Skills (Definition List)

Child Protection and Safeguarding

This work focuses on ensuring that children grow up in safe environments at home, in school, in church, and in the community. It involves identifying risks that may harm a child and establishing systems that prevent abuse, neglect, or exploitation. Awareness is raised among parents, teachers, and leaders so that they understand their responsibility in protecting children. Institutions are supported to develop safeguarding policies and procedures that guide safe practice. When concerns arise, appropriate steps are taken to respond carefully and protect the child while working with responsible authorities.

Counselling and Psychological Support

This area focuses on supporting the emotional and psychological well being of individuals and families. Many people face stress, trauma, or difficult life circumstances that affect relationships and daily functioning. Counselling provides a safe and respectful space where individuals can express their concerns and receive guidance. Through active listening and supportive dialogue, people are encouraged to develop healthy coping strategies and rebuild hope and confidence.

Community Mobilization and Engagement

Community mobilization involves bringing people together to address issues that affect children and families. Local leaders, parents, teachers, and youth are engaged through meetings, training sessions, and awareness activities. The aim is to help communities recognize risks facing children and develop collective solutions that reflect their cultural and social context. When communities are informed and united, they become strong partners in safeguarding children.

Leadership and Team Management

Leadership involves guiding individuals and teams toward a shared purpose. Effective leaders communicate clearly, mentor others, and create environments where people feel valued and motivated. Team management includes planning activities, coordinating roles, and ensuring collaboration among members. Strong leadership encourages accountability, integrity, and service oriented work that benefits communities.

Monitoring and Evaluation

Monitoring and evaluation help organizations understand whether their programs are achieving meaningful results. Information is collected and analyzed to track progress and assess impact. The process highlights successes and identifies areas that require improvement. This evidence supports better decision making and ensures that programs continue to serve children, families, and communities effectively.
